Who am I?

My name is Mehrdad Najapfour (مهرداد نجف‌پور). I am currently a Postdoctoral Researcher at the Laboratory of Advanced Biotechnologies for Health Assessment (LAB-HA) within the Department of Electrical Engineering and Computer Science, Lassonde School of Engineering, York University, Toronto, Canada, under the supervision of Dr. Neda Salahandish.

I obtained my Ph.D. degree in 2024 from the Université du Québec à Montréal (UQAM), Canada, under the supervision of Dr. Vestislav Apostolov and Dr. Frédéric Rochon. My mathematical genealogy can be found here: Mathematics Genealogy Project.

My research interests include Geometric Deep Learning, Geometric Analysis, Partial Differential Equations (PDEs), Applied Statistics, Statistical Learning, and Mathematical Biology. Within these areas, I work as an AI/ML Algorithm and Statistical Modeling Expert, developing mathematically grounded methods that connect theoretical insights with real-world applications.

I am passionate about teaching through a mathematics-based approach and take pride in helping learners understand and appreciate the inherent beauty of mathematics. My enthusiasm for education is complemented by a strong dedication to mathematical research. My goal is to lead innovative, interdisciplinary projects at the intersection of mathematical theory, artificial intelligence, and practical applications.

Outside the world of mathematics and AI, I am an amateur reader of history and literature.

I welcome opportunities to connect and collaborate.
